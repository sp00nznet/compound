# QQQM — Invesco Nasdaq-100 ETF (DEMO research report)

> ⚠️ DEMO / illustrative. Generated for a fictional ~$10,000 demo account. Not real, not investment advice.

## Snapshot
- Tracks the **Nasdaq-100** — the 100 largest non-financial companies on the Nasdaq
- Low expense ratio (~0.15%) · the cheaper "buy-and-hold" twin of QQQ
- Heavily weighted to mega-cap technology

## What it is / what it holds
QQQM owns the same index as the well-known QQQ but at a lower fee, aimed at long-term holders. It's dominated by the largest tech names (Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia, Amazon, etc.), so it's a concentrated bet on big tech and growth — by design.

## Performance & behavior
The Nasdaq-100 has been a top performer in tech-led bull markets and a hard faller in tech selloffs (e.g., 2022). High return, high volatility — expect big swings.

## Costs & taxes
~0.15% fee — higher than a broad S&P 500 fund but low in absolute terms. Standard equity-ETF tax treatment.

## Bull case
- Spreads tech exposure across 100 names instead of betting on one or two stocks.
- Cheap, liquid, one trade.
- Captures the AI/big-tech theme if it keeps running.

## Bear case / key risks
- **Still concentrated in mega-cap tech** — it overlaps the demo book's existing AAPL/MSFT/NVDA/TSLA, so it is *not* true diversification.
- High volatility; deep drawdowns in tech corrections.
- Adding it on top of an already tech-heavy book makes concentration worse.

## Role in this (demo) portfolio
Treat QQQM as a **swap, not new money**: if you want to keep heavy tech conviction, owning the 100-name basket spreads single-stock blow-up risk versus betting on NVDA + TSLA. It does **not** reduce the portfolio's overall tech concentration — so it's only sensible *in place of* single tech names, not in addition to them.

## Alternatives & how to choose
- **QQQ** — same index, higher fee, more options liquidity (for traders).
- **VGT / XLK** — broad tech-sector funds, slightly different mix.
- Choose QQQM for low-cost long-term Nasdaq-100 exposure.

## Verdict & suggested action
Optional, and only as a quality swap for single tech names — not a diversifier. If you keep strong tech conviction, swap single names into QQQM rather than adding tech on top.
