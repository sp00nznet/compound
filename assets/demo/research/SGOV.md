# SGOV — iShares 0–3 Month Treasury Bond ETF (DEMO research report)

> ⚠️ DEMO / illustrative. Generated for a fictional ~$10,000 demo account. Not real, not investment advice.

## Snapshot
- Holds only ultra-short US Treasury bills (0–3 months to maturity)
- ~3.5–3.9% yield · expense ratio ~0.09% · pays monthly
- Price barely moves — effectively "cash that pays interest"

## What it is / what it holds
SGOV owns the shortest, safest IOUs the US government issues. Because each bill matures within weeks, its price stays in a tight band — it drifts up as interest accrues, then drops on the payout date. Your return arrives as a **monthly cash distribution**, not as price gains.

## Yield & the Fed
SGOV's payout tracks the Federal Reserve's short-term rate in near-real time. Rates high → it pays well; **Fed cuts → the payout falls within weeks.** You get today's rate with almost no price risk, but no lock-in if rates drop.

## Costs & taxes
~0.09% fee (negligible). US Treasury interest is exempt from **state and local** income tax (you still owe federal) — a real edge over a same-rate bank savings account in a high-tax state.

## Bull case
- Cash that actually pays (~3.5–3.9%) vs ~0% sitting idle.
- Backed by the US government, ultra-short — about as safe as a market asset gets.
- Highly liquid; sell any market day.

## Bear case / key risks
- Income shrinks if the Fed cuts rates.
- Not growth — it will trail stocks badly over the long run.
- Inflation can still erode purchasing power if it runs above the yield.

## Role in this (demo) portfolio
Turns idle cash into earning **dry powder** and acts as ballast — it barely moves when stocks fall, and gives you ready money to buy dips.

## Alternatives & how to choose
- **BIL** — near-identical, slightly higher fee.
- **USFR** — floating-rate Treasuries, similar use.
- **HYSA / money-market fund** — similar yield, no trade needed, but usually no state-tax break.

## Verdict & suggested action
A safe, low-stakes cash sleeve. Suggested: ~$250 of the freed cash. Understand the income drifts down if the Fed eases.
