# VXUS — Vanguard Total International Stock ETF (DEMO research report)

> ⚠️ DEMO / illustrative. Generated for a fictional ~$10,000 demo account. Not real, not investment advice.

## Snapshot
- One ticker = thousands of companies **outside the US** (developed + emerging markets)
- Very low expense ratio (~0.05–0.08%) · pays a modest dividend
- The geographic diversification a US-only portfolio lacks

## What it is / what it holds
VXUS holds the international slice of the global stock market — Europe, Japan, emerging markets like India and Taiwan, and more — across thousands of names, weighted by size. It deliberately excludes US stocks, so it's designed to sit *alongside* a US fund, not replace it.

## Performance & behavior
International stocks have lagged the US for much of the past decade, but they don't always move together — different regions, currencies, and cycles. Valuations abroad are often cheaper than the US, which some investors see as a setup for better future returns (not a guarantee).

## Costs & taxes
Rock-bottom fee. Foreign dividends may have foreign tax withheld; in a taxable US account you can often claim a foreign tax credit. Dividends are taxable in the year received.

## Bull case
- The only holding here that isn't US-dollar / US-economy dependent.
- Cheaper valuations than the US on average.
- True diversification — different drivers than your US names.

## Bear case / key risks
- Has underperformed the US for years; that can persist.
- Currency (FX) risk — a strong dollar drags returns.
- Includes weaker-governance and emerging markets with extra political risk.

## Role in this (demo) portfolio
The demo book is 100% US. VXUS is the geographic spread — it reduces the risk that a US-specific shock hits everything you own at once.

## Alternatives & how to choose
- **VEU** — similar, excludes small caps.
- **IXUS** — iShares equivalent.
- **VEA + VWO** — split developed vs emerging if you want to control the mix.

## Verdict & suggested action
A reasonable diversifier for a US-only book. Suggested: ~$170 of the freed cash. Size it as a satellite, not a core bet, given the long US outperformance streak.
