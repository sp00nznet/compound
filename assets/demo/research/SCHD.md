# SCHD — Schwab US Dividend Equity ETF (DEMO research report)

> ⚠️ DEMO / illustrative. Generated for a fictional ~$10,000 demo account. Not real, not investment advice.

## Snapshot
- ETF (a basket of stocks bought as one ticker) of ~100 established US dividend-paying companies
- Low expense ratio (~0.06%) · dividend yield typically ~3–4%
- Tilts toward consumer staples, health care, industrials, energy — the opposite end from mega-cap growth

## What it is / what it holds
SCHD screens the US market for companies with a consistent dividend history plus financial strength (cash flow, low debt, return on equity), then weights them toward quality and yield. The result is a value-leaning, income-paying basket that behaves very differently from a tech-heavy growth portfolio.

## Performance & behavior
SCHD trades growth for resilience: it usually lags in tech-led bull markets and holds up better in selloffs, while paying you a growing dividend along the way. Total return over time has been competitive with the broad market with lower drawdowns, though past results don't predict the future.

## Costs & taxes
Very low fee (~0.06%). Dividends are taxable in the year received (qualified dividends get the lower rate); hold in a tax-advantaged account if you can.

## Bull case
- Diversifies a growth/tech-heavy book toward value and income.
- Pays you ~3–4% while you wait; dividends have grown over time.
- Tends to fall less in market stress — a smoother ride.

## Bear case / key risks
- Lags badly in tech-driven rallies (it deliberately underweights them).
- Rate-sensitive (dividend stocks compete with bonds).
- Not a growth engine — won't compound like the Nasdaq in a tech bull run.

## Role in this (demo) portfolio
The demo book is ~61% big-tech. SCHD is the cleanest counterweight: it adds value, dividends, and defensive sectors the portfolio completely lacks.

## Alternatives & how to choose
- **VYM** (Vanguard High Dividend Yield) — broader, more names, similar idea.
- **DGRO** (iShares Core Dividend Growth) — tilts to dividend *growth* over raw yield.
- Choose SCHD for the quality screen + low fee; VYM for breadth; DGRO for growth-of-income.

## Verdict & suggested action
A sensible core diversifier for a growth-heavy demo book. Suggested: ~$300 of the freed cash, added gradually.
