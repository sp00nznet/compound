Plain-English glossary

Moat — a durable advantage that keeps competitors from stealing a company's profits (brand, scale, network, switching costs).
ETF — Exchange-Traded Fund: a basket of many stocks (or bonds) bought as one ticker. One trade = instant diversification.
P/E (price-to-earnings) — stock price ÷ yearly profit per share. Higher = more expensive / more growth priced in.
Dividend yield — yearly cash dividend as a % of price.
REIT — Real Estate Investment Trust: a company that owns income property and pays most of its profit out as dividends.
Concentration — how much of your money is in one stock or theme. High = big reward if right, big loss if wrong.
Correlation — how much holdings move together. Your tech names tend to rise and fall as a group.
Dry powder — cash kept ready to buy opportunities or cushion a drop.
Trim vs sell — trim = sell part of a position; sell = exit it entirely.
Position sizing — how big each holding is relative to the whole. Often higher-impact than which stock you pick.
