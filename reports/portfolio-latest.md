## Step 3: Single-Position Health Check

### Tencent Holdings — If You Held None Today, Would You Still Buy?

**Yes.**

Reasons:
- The WeChat ecosystem (1.3B users) is China's strongest digital infrastructure, with an extremely deep moat
- Non-IFRS PE of ~15–16x is reasonable-to-cheap for a platform company growing 14% a year and buying back stock at scale
- Abundant free cash flow; shareholder return (buybacks + dividends) yields ~2.5–3% annually
- AI transformation is advancing steadily — not aggressive, but not falling behind

> Duan Yongping: "Tencent is the kind of company you can watch for ten years and nothing goes wrong."

**Comfortable holding for 5 years?** Very comfortable. WeChat's network effects are nearly irreversible, with a multi-engine drive of gaming + advertising + fintech. The only concern is the uncertainty of regulatory policy.

**Health**: ★★★★★

### Pinduoduo — If You Held None Today, Would You Still Buy?

**Yes, but I'd want a bigger margin of safety.**

Reasons:
- 10x PE for a still-growing e-commerce platform is very attractive
- The domestic Pinduoduo platform's position is solid, with deeply ingrained low-price mindshare
- Temu's overseas expansion is huge option value, but also huge uncertainty

> Munger: "The companies we bought cheaply in adversity have often given us our best returns."

**Comfortable holding for 5 years?** Somewhat uneasy. Temu faces tariff barriers (the U.S. ending the de minimis exemption for small parcels) and the risk of regulatory crackdowns in various countries. Domestic e-commerce competition is fierce (Douyin e-commerce, JD's low-price strategy). Management is deliberately sacrificing profit to invest, pressuring near-term results.

**Health**: ★★★☆☆

### Pop Mart — If You Held None Today, Would You Still Buy?

**Attractive at the current price, but requires caution.**

Reasons:
- Down from 339 to 141.80, the valuation has come back from unbelievable to acceptable (13.1x PE, 57% ROE)
- Globalization is showing early results, with overseas revenue at 44%
- The IP matrix, such as LABUBU, has extremely strong explosive power

But:
- Designer toys are essentially a "fashion" business; IP lifecycles are uncertain
- 2026 growth guidance cut to 20%; the high-growth story may be winding down
- Founding shareholders cashing out is a negative signal

> Li Lu: "For a consumer company, the most important thing is whether brand loyalty can survive across economic cycles. Pop Mart still needs time to prove it."

**Comfortable holding for 5 years?** Not entirely sure. If the IP matrix keeps iterating and overseas expansion goes smoothly, this could be a remarkable consumer company. But the risk of the designer-toy trend turning cannot be ignored.

**Health**: ★★★☆☆

**Assessment**: Concentration itself isn't the problem; the problem is that all three names are Chinese companies. This isn't "concentrated in good companies," it's "concentrated in single-country risk."

**Key findings**:

1. **Single-country China risk exposure = 85%** — this is the portfolio's biggest structural problem. Any systemic event involving China (a regulatory storm, geopolitical conflict, economic recession) would hit all three positions simultaneously.

2. **Internet-sector exposure = 65%** — both Tencent and Pinduoduo are internet companies, facing common regulatory risks (antitrust, data security) and industry risks (traffic growth peaking).

3. **Consumer sensitivity = 50–65%** — both Pinduoduo and Pop Mart depend heavily on consumer willingness to spend. A weaker-than-expected China consumption recovery would hit both at once.

> Munger: "The real risk isn't volatility, it's the permanent loss of capital. When all your eggs are in one basket and that basket drops..."

## Step 6: Conclusion

### Portfolio Health: ★★★☆☆ (3/5)

**Strengths**:
- All three companies have decent fundamentals; valuations are reasonable overall
- Tencent is an excellent ballast
- Cash level is moderate

**Problems**:
- Geographic concentration is too high (100% China) — a fatal structural flaw
- Pinduoduo's risk/reward doesn't match its position weight
- Pop Mart's long-term certainty isn't enough to support a 20% position

### The One Thing Most Worth Doing Now

**Trim Pinduoduo by 10 percentage points and allocate to high-quality non-China assets.**

This single step solves three problems at once:
1. Reduces tail-risk exposure to U.S.–China conflict
2. Lessens over-concentration in the internet sector
3. Adds geographic diversification

### The Biggest Current Risk

**Single-country China risk.** 85% of the portfolio is exposed to one country's regulatory/macro/geopolitical environment. This isn't investing in three different companies — it's making one giant concentrated bet on "China's economy + China's internet + China's consumption."

> Duan Yongping: "Good investing is, first of all, surviving. Spreading risk a little isn't about being timid — it's because we don't know what tomorrow holds."
